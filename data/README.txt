Data on opioid- and stimulant-related harms in Canada

======================================

The overdose crisis has affected every part of the country, but there are clear differences in crude rates and numbers across provinces and territories.

The national-level data contained in this report are based on available data reported as of February 4, 2025.

Authors: Public Health Agency of Canada (PHAC) and provincial and territorial (PT) offices of Chief Coroners and Chief Medical Examiners as well as PT public health and health partners and Emergency Medical Services (EMS) data providers. Canadian Institute for Health Information (CIHI) collected and provided the data used for reporting opioid- and stimulant-related poisoning hospitalizations and emergency department visits.

File
-----

* The SubstanceHarmsData.csv contains opioid- and stimulant-related harms (mortality, hospitalizations, emergency department visits, and emergency medical services responses) data in Canada by province and territory.


Note
----- 
Differences may exist in case identification and reporting, type of data reported and time period of available data. Comparisons over time and between provinces and territories should be interpreted with caution. Refer to technical notes for more information: https://health-infobase.canada.ca/substance-related-harms/opioids-stimulants/technical-notes
